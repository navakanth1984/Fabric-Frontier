## Details
• Huffman coding is a lossless data compression algorithm that can be used to compress the weights of neural networks.
• Compressed weights can lead to faster training times and reduced memory requirements, making deep learning more accessible.
• Research papers on this topic explore the use of Huffman coding in various neural network architectures, including convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs).
• Some papers also investigate the trade-offs between compression ratio and computational overhead.
